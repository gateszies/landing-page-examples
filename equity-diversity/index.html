Fighting the coronavirus recession

The Ipsum Center for Equity and Diversity will host a virtual lecture on Monday, June 15 led by Lipsum Donor, director of inclusion policy at Ipsum Center. We'll discuss promising research ideas that support a robust response to fight the coronavirus recession, as well as longer-term efforts to ensure a more resilient U.S. economy in the years to come.

Learn More
Contact Us

When: Monday, June 15, 2020, 2-3 PM CST

Where: Online. The event is invite-only and instructions on how to participate will be sent after registration is confirmed.

Lipsum will build upon evidence in her book, with a focus on policies that stabilize the economy through supporting families, small businesses, and communities. Her presentation will be followed by a conversation with Dolor Sit president & CEO of Lorem Ipsum, on various themes, including the role of fiscal and monetary policy to protect workers and their families, as well as the importance of diversity, equity, and inclusion within the field of macroeconomics.

This event will be part of a new series that seeks a deeper understanding of cutting-edge research and analysis on economic inequality and growth. Our lectures will bring together leading scholars to explore how new research is shifting important conversations in academia and economic policy.
